# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Australian businesses running safety, risk, compliance and ISO certification programs — WHS/GRC managers, site supervisors, and business owners in construction, trades and similar site-based industries. They're currently juggling spreadsheets, paper pre-starts and multiple point tools, and are evaluating whether Briesa can replace that sprawl before an auditor's visit. Visitors arrive skeptical of compliance-software vendors and need to be convinced the platform is both rigorous enough to survive an ISO/WHS audit and simple enough for field workers to actually use daily.

## Product Purpose

Briesa is a unified platform for WHS, GRC and ISO certification (45001/14001/9001) for Australian businesses — from the daily site pre-start to the auditor's evidence pack. The marketing site's job is to get a qualified visitor to request a demo or join the waitlist by making the platform's rigor and day-to-day usability legible at a glance. Success = demo requests / waitlist signups from businesses that recognise their own compliance pain in the site's copy and product previews.

## Brand Personality

Confident, modern, sharp. The site should read as audit-grade and precise, not playful or soft — but it should also feel like a tech-forward product, not legacy enterprise GRC software. Comfortable with bolder, more saturated color and punchier motion than a typical "restrained SaaS" default, as long as it stays purposeful rather than decorative.

## Anti-references

Keep the existing visual identity intact — dark-first, zero-radius, editorial typography (Space Grotesk + IBM Plex Mono), single-accent discipline. The ask is to extend this system with more deliberate color and interaction, not replace it with a generic bubbly-gradient SaaS look or lapse into dated, form-heavy legacy compliance-software aesthetics.

## Design Principles

- **Color carries meaning, not decoration.** Every new highlight color should map to something real (a status, a framework, a category) — never applied just to break up monochrome.
- **Confidence through precision, not noise.** Sharp zero-radius edges, tight type, and restrained layout signal audit-grade rigor; boldness comes from committed color and motion choices, not clutter.
- **Evidence over adjectives.** Prefer showing real dashboard data, scores and trends (as the existing dashboard preview does) over vague marketing claims.
- **Motion respects the reader.** Reveals and micro-interactions are purposeful and tied to what they reveal; every animation ships a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback that keeps content visible.
- **Local trust signals stay prominent.** WHS Act 2011, SafeWork NSW, ISO badges, "data hosted in Australia" — these are conversion-critical and shouldn't be visually diluted by new color/motion work.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, full keyboard navigation, and `prefers-reduced-motion` support for all animation (already implemented via a CSS fallback in globals.css).
